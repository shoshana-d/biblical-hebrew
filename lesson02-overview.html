Lesson 2

Overview How Tanakh Hebrew is different from English

Summary

  • A single Hebrew word corresponds to 2 or more English words in the translation

    this occurs for 2 main reasons:

    • Some English words are not separate words in Tanakh Hebrew. In Tanakh Hebrew, these "words" are added as prefixes (at the beginning of a Hebrew word) or suffixes (at the end of a word).

      Prefix "words": and, the, to, in, from, like/as

      Suffix words: my, your, his, her, our, their, me, you, him, us, them

    • Hebrew verbs are always a single word and they contain much more information than English verbs which often have a "helper" verb
  • Order of words is different, in particular, often verb first
  • the Hebrew word et which has no English equivalent
  • No Tanakh Hebrew word for of, when 2 nouns together, translated as though "of" between them
  • sentences without verbs
  • hyphens
