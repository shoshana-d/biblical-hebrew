Prefixes and suffixes

Some English words are not separate words in Tanakh Hebrew. In Tanakh Hebrew, these "words" are added as prefixes (at the beginning of a Hebrew word) or suffixes (at the end of a word). For example:

Prefixes examples

עִירwords/w039_ir

city, town

הָעִיר

the city

וְעִיר

and a city

וְהָעִיר

and the city

לְעִיר

to a city

בְּעִיר

in a city

מֵעִיר

from a city

כְּעִיר

like a city

Suffixes examples

עִירwords/w039_ir

city, town

עִירוֹ

his city

עִירָם

their city

עִירִי

my city

עִירָהּ

her city

Both prefix and suffix

עִירwords/w039_ir

city, town

מֵעִירוֹ

from his city

So a single Hebrew word often corresponds to two or more English words.
