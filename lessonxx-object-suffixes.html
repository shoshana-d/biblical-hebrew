Suffixes (at the end of a preposition): me, you, him, her, us, them, it (object pronouns)

all versions with l'? and include obects on verbs and oto etc??

  • These are examples of prepositions: to, toward, in, at, by, with, against, into, on, according to, as, upon, on account of
  • When the object pronouns me, you, him, her, us, them occur after a preposition, the pronoun is always a suffix added to the END of the preposition.

So when you see, for example, "to him", "to her", "from them", "by me" or "to you" in the English translation, this will always be one word in the Hebrew.
