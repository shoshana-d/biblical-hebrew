Words for God, names of people and places

Names of people and places are called proper nouns. In English, they start with a capital letter (Moses, Egypt) but there are no capital letters in Hebrew. When trying to decipher a verse, the first thing you should look at is whether there are any proper names, and if so, to find them in the Hebrew.

Names of God in the Tanakh

יהוה

In the Tanakh, the word יהוה refers to God. יהוה is the most frequently occurring word on the Tanakh (as you would expect). יהוה is called the Tetragrammaton (which is Greek for "four letters"). Jewish practice is to pronounce this word as אֲדוֹנָיwords/w062_adonai (Adonai, my Lord). יהוה is usually pointed יְהוָה so the vowels are similar to אֲדוֹנָיwords/w062_adonai See How to pronounce יְהוָה the Tetragrammaton in Learn alefbet for more information on the pronunciation of this word.

translations of יהוה God, GOD, the LORD, L-rd, Lord, Adonoy, untranslated, Yhwh

pronouns relating to God (His, Him) also start with a capital letter in most translations
