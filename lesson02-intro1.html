How Tanakh verbs work

וַיֹּאמֶרwords/w011_vayomer is a verb.

To start with, because we are working backwards from the English translation, we need to know some things about verbs, and particularly, how verbs in Hebrew are different from verbs in English

As a reminder about what verbs are, here is the translation of Genesis chapter 16 verse 6 with the verbs highlighted:

Abram said to Sarai, "Your maid is in your hands. Deal with her as you think right. Then Sarai treated her harshly, and she ran away from her.

  • In English, the verb usually comes AFTER the person performing the action (we call this person the subject).
    • Abram said
      Abram
      subject
      said
      verb
    • your maid is
    • you think
    • Sarai treated
    • she ran
  • In Tanakh Hebrew the word order is often verb-subject, that is, the verb often comes before the subject
    • said
      verb
      Abram
      subject

The verb Deal doesn't have a subject, this is a different form of the verb, Abram is directly addressing Sarai. All the other forms are descriptions.

The verb וַיֹּאמֶרwords/w011_vayomer always comes before the subject.

Can often use this to figure out words you don't know.
