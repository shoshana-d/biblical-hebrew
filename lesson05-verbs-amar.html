Vayomer can have lots of English translations

from intro

Because the Tanakh has a much smaller vocbulary than English, a Biblical Hebrew word often has several different English translations

Good - less vocab to learn, AND learning the most frequent means yourecognize a large percentage of text

Bad, the most frequent words often have multiple translations, so need to be a detective

same hebrew word often translated differently according to context. Often this is because of the small vocab in the Tanakh

וַיֹּאמֶרwords/w011_vayomer is a verb. Hebrew verb is always only one word but translation may be 2 or even 3 words. Because Hebrew verb contains all the information on person and "tense" Gen 42:9 Recalling the dream that -he had dreamed- about them Short Introduction to how verbs work – 3 letter root, prefixes (example) and suffixes (example), in English only suffixes (plays, played, playing) prefixes and suffixes to show person don’t need a subject specified root א מ ר y' tells us that one male person is speaking t' tells us that one female person is speaking וַתֹּאמֶרwords/w011_vatomer Dont' need the subject - examples when "he said", "she said"
